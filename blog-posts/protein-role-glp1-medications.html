The Critical Role of Protein While on GLP-1 Medications

By Michelle Michael, RD, CEO & Founder
January 11, 2025
25 min read

If you're currently using a GLP-1 receptor agonist medication – perhaps known by brand names like Ozempic®, Wegovy®, Mounjaro®, Zepbound®, Saxenda®, Victoza®, or Rybelsus® – making adequate protein intake a priority is not just beneficial; it's critical for harnessing the full potential of these medications while minimizing potential downsides.

Why Protein Takes Center Stage on GLP-1s

Preserving Your Strength: Combating Muscle Loss

Studies looking at GLP-1 induced weight loss report varying amounts of lean mass loss, sometimes estimated between 20% and 50% of total weight lost. This muscle loss can impact your metabolism, strength, and long-term success.

Why Preserving Muscle Matters:

  • Maintains higher metabolic rate
  • Supports strength and daily function
  • Promotes healthier body composition
  • Protects bones and joints

Feeling Fuller, Longer: Protein Power for Satiety

Protein is the most satiating macronutrient, helping you feel fuller for longer. This effect works synergistically with GLP-1 medications to enhance and extend feelings of fullness.

Meeting Your Protein Needs: Personalized Strategies

Understanding Your Unique Protein Target

General recommendations often suggest:

  • 1.2 to 1.6 grams of protein per kilogram of ideal body weight
  • 25-30 grams of protein per meal
  • Regular protein-rich snacks

Smart Strategies for Success:

  • Eat protein first at meals
  • Spread intake throughout the day
  • Choose high-quality sources
  • Consider protein supplements if needed

Navigating Challenges: When Appetite is Low

Practical Tips:

  • Try softer protein sources
  • Experiment with food temperatures
  • Use protein powders in smoothies
  • Eat smaller, more frequent meals

Your Protein Plan for Success

Prioritizing protein while using GLP-1 medications helps preserve muscle mass, enhance satiety, support blood sugar stability, and promote optimal body composition. While it requires conscious effort, especially when facing challenges like reduced appetite, the benefits are substantial for your long-term health and success.

Important Note: Personalized Guidance

Because individual needs vary greatly, discuss your personal protein requirements with your healthcare provider and consider working with a Registered Dietitian for personalized nutrition guidance tailored to your specific needs and goals.
