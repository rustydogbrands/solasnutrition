Understanding GLP-1 Medications: How They Work and Nutritional Implications

By Michelle Michael, RD, CEO & Founder
April 14, 2025
25 min read

In recent years, a class of medications known as GLP-1 receptor agonists (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 receptor agonists) has become increasingly prominent in healthcare discussions across the United States. Drugs like Semaglutide (found in Ozempic and Wegovy) and Liraglutide (found in Victoza and Saxenda) are frequently mentioned, alongside newer dual-action agents like Tirzepatide (found in Mounjaro and Zepbound), which target both GLP-1 and GIP (Glucose-dependent Insulinotropic Polypeptide) receptors.

Part 1: Demystifying GLP-1 Medications - How They Work

Meet Your Body's Natural Helpers: GLP-1 and GIP Hormones

To understand how these medications work, it's essential to understand the natural hormones they interact with. The body produces several hormones in the gut in response to eating, known as incretin hormones, which play crucial roles in regulating blood sugar and appetite.

Key Functions of Natural GLP-1:

  • Stimulates insulin release when blood sugar is high
  • Reduces glucagon secretion
  • Slows down gastric emptying
  • Controls appetite through brain signaling

How GLP-1 Medications Step In (Mimicking Nature)

GLP-1 receptor agonist medications are engineered molecules designed to mimic the actions of natural GLP-1 but are resistant to breakdown by DPP-4. This allows them to last much longer in the body â€“ from hours to a full week, depending on the specific drug.

Part 2: Connecting the Dots - Nutritional Implications

Because Your Stomach Empties Slower...

Common Side Effects:

  • Nausea
  • Early satiety
  • Bloating
  • Acid reflux
  • Digestive changes

Nutritional Strategies to Manage These Effects:

  • Eat smaller, more frequent meals
  • Reduce high-fat foods
  • Eat slowly and chew thoroughly
  • Stay upright after eating
  • Listen to fullness cues
  • Hydrate between meals

Because Your Appetite Changes...

The significant reduction in hunger and overall food intake presents a potential challenge: the risk of undernutrition. If calorie intake becomes too low without careful planning, individuals may not consume sufficient amounts of essential nutrients, including protein, vitamins, and minerals.

Making Every Bite Count:

  • Focus on nutrient-dense foods
  • Prioritize protein (aim for 60-100g+ daily)
  • Ensure adequate hydration
  • Include fiber-rich foods
  • Don't forget healthy fats

Key Takeaways

Understanding how GLP-1 medications work in your body is crucial for maximizing their benefits while managing potential challenges. By implementing targeted nutritional strategies that address the specific effects of these medications, you can better support your health goals and optimize your treatment success.

Essential Next Steps: Talk to Your Healthcare Team

  • Discuss medication effects and management with your prescribing provider
  • Consult a Registered Dietitian for personalized nutrition guidance
  • Monitor and report any persistent side effects
