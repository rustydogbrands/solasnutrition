Your Delicious Guide to Dining Out with Confidence on GLP-1 Medications

By Michelle Michael, RD, CEO & Founder
April 13, 2025
20 min read

Let's face it, dining out is woven into the fabric of American life. It's where we connect with friends and family, celebrate milestones, grab a convenient bite on busy days, or simply explore new flavors and experiences. While using GLP-1 medications may change how your body responds to food, it doesn't mean you have to give up the joy of dining out.

Understanding Your New Normal

If you're using a GLP-1 receptor agonist medication – like Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, or Zepbound – you're likely experiencing some changes in how your body handles food.

How GLP-1s Affect Eating:

  • Slow down stomach emptying
  • Increase satiety signals
  • Reduce appetite and hunger cues

Phase 1: Before You Go - Smart Planning & Preparation

Become a Menu Detective

What to Look For:

  • Keywords for lighter options: grilled, baked, broiled, steamed
  • Lean protein choices: chicken breast, fish, shrimp
  • Vegetable variety: non-starchy options, salads
  • Potential pitfalls: fried, crispy, creamy, cheesy

Timing & Pre-Meal Strategy

  • Don't skip meals to "save up" for dinner
  • Consider a small protein snack if needed
  • Stay hydrated throughout the day

Phase 2: At the Restaurant - Ordering Like a Pro

GLP-1 Friendly Restaurant Choices
Category Favor Limit or Avoid
Proteins Grilled/baked fish, chicken breast, lean steak Fried meats, fatty cuts, processed meats
Vegetables Steamed, roasted, grilled veggies Creamed or cheese-sauce vegetables
Carbohydrates Small portions of whole grains Large pasta/rice portions, french fries

Smart Ordering Tips

  • Ask for sauces and dressings on the side
  • Request modifications (steamed instead of sautéed)
  • Consider appetizers as main courses
  • Don't hesitate to ask questions about preparation

Phase 3: At the Table - Mastering Portions & Pace

The Proactive To-Go Box Strategy

As soon as your meal arrives, request a to-go container and portion out what you'll likely want to save before you start eating. This prevents the urge to overeat and acknowledges your body's new signals.

Mindful Eating Techniques:

  • Eat slowly and deliberately
  • Put utensils down between bites
  • Chew thoroughly
  • Check in with hunger/fullness signals
  • Stop when comfortably satisfied

Handling Social Situations Gracefully

Polite Responses to Comments:

  • "Everything looks amazing! I just get full quite quickly these days."
  • "Thank you, it was delicious, but I'm perfectly satisfied."
  • "I'm really savoring this, so I'm taking my time."

Your Toolkit for Success

Dining out while using GLP-1 medications is absolutely achievable with the right strategies. Remember to plan ahead, make smart choices, manage portions, and most importantly, continue to enjoy the social aspects of sharing meals with others.

Important Note: Your Health Partners

While this guide offers practical tips, your experience with GLP-1 medication is unique to you. If you consistently find dining out difficult or have concerns about nutrition, please discuss these with your healthcare provider and consider consulting with a Registered Dietitian for personalized guidance.
