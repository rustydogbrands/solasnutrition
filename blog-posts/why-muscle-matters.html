Why Muscle Matters: Preventing Muscle Loss on GLP-1 Medications

By Michelle Michael, RD, CEO & Founder
February 3, 2025
25 min read

While GLP-1 receptor agonists represent a significant advancement in obesity and type 2 diabetes management, the substantial weight loss they promote carries a potential risk: the loss of valuable muscle mass alongside fat. Understanding why muscle preservation matters and implementing strategies to protect it is crucial for long-term health outcomes.

The Critical Importance of Muscle Mass

Your Metabolic Engine: Fueling Sustainable Results

Skeletal muscle is far more than just tissue for movement; it is a metabolically active organ that plays a crucial role in energy expenditure. Constituting approximately 40% of body weight, muscle tissue burns significantly more calories at rest compared to fat tissue, contributing about 30% of the body's resting energy expenditure.

Why Preserving Muscle Matters:

  • Maintains higher metabolic rate
  • Supports physical strength and function
  • Improves insulin sensitivity
  • Promotes bone health
  • Enhances overall well-being

GLP-1 Medications and the Muscle Loss Connection

Studies have reported that up to 40% or even 50% of total weight reduction during GLP-1 therapy could be lean body mass in certain cases. This significant muscle loss isn't a direct effect of the medication but rather a consequence of reduced calorie intake and potentially inadequate protein consumption.

Your Action Plan: Strategies for Muscle Preservation

1. Fuel Your Muscles: Prioritize Protein

Aim for 1.2 to 1.6 grams of protein per kilogram of ideal body weight daily, distributed across meals. Quality protein sources include lean meats, fish, eggs, dairy, and plant-based options.

Protein Timing Tips:

  • Eat protein first at meals
  • Distribute intake across 3-4 meals
  • Aim for 20-40g per serving
  • Consider protein supplements if needed

2. Signal Your Muscles: Engage in Resistance Training

Resistance training provides the essential stimulus signaling the body to preserve and build muscle tissue. Aim for 2-4 sessions per week targeting all major muscle groups.

3. Monitor Your Progress: Beyond the Scale

Track progress using multiple methods beyond weight alone:

  • Strength gains in workouts
  • Body measurements
  • Progress photos
  • How clothes fit
  • Energy levels and well-being

Taking Charge of Your Health

Successfully preserving muscle while using GLP-1 medications requires a proactive approach combining adequate protein intake, consistent resistance training, and proper recovery. This investment in muscle health supports not just weight management, but overall health and quality of life.

CRUCIAL NEXT STEP: Talk to Your Healthcare Team

Before starting any new exercise program or making significant changes to your diet while on GLP-1 medications, consult with:

  • Your prescribing healthcare provider
  • A Registered Dietitian for personalized nutrition guidance
  • A qualified fitness professional for exercise programming
